Market Analysis and Performance Indicators

Introduction

This guide provides detailed explanations of the key metrics and insights used in our market analysis dashboards. It is designed to help decision-makers understand the technical aspects of our market performance indicators and their business implications.

Each metric is explained in terms of:

  • What it measures
  • How it's calculated
  • How to interpret different values
  • Business implications and actionable insights
  • Practical examples where applicable

The metrics are organized into categories that align with our dashboard structure, making it easy to reference when reviewing market performance.

Brand Health Metrics

Brand health metrics measure consumer perceptions and attitudes toward our destination. These metrics are leading indicators that typically precede changes in actual travel behavior.

Awareness

86.89% (+3.09pp)

Awareness measures the percentage of the target audience who are aware of Abu Dhabi as a Cultural and Sea & Sun travel destination.

How It's Calculated:

Based on survey responses to questions like "Which international holiday destinations offering a combination of both cultural and sun & beach activities are you aware of?" The percentage represents those who respond positively out of the total surveyed population.

Interpretation:
  • 90%+: Excellent awareness, approaching market saturation
  • 70-90%: Strong awareness, but room for improvement
  • 50-70%: Moderate awareness, significant opportunity for growth
  • <50%: Low awareness, major opportunity but requires substantial investment
Business Implications:
  • At 86.89%, Qatar has very strong awareness of Abu Dhabi, indicating excellent market penetration.
  • The +3.09pp year-over-year growth suggests continued improvement in awareness levels.
  • The awareness level is approaching the 90% threshold, which would indicate market saturation.
  • Marketing should maintain awareness-building efforts while increasingly focusing on conversion to consideration and intent.
  • The positive trend in awareness provides a solid foundation for more targeted marketing efforts aimed at improving consideration and intent metrics.
  • Given the proximity of Qatar to Abu Dhabi, this high awareness level is expected but still represents a strong foundation for marketing efforts.

Consideration

43.67% (+2.67pp)

Consideration measures the percentage of aware consumers who would consider travelling to Abu Dhabi in the next 3 years.

How It's Calculated:

Based on survey responses to questions like "Which of the below holiday destinations would you like to visit in the next 3 years?" The percentage represents those who respond positively out of those who are aware of Abu Dhabi.

Interpretation:
  • 70%+: Excellent consideration, strong position in consumer mindset
  • 50-70%: Good consideration, competitive position
  • 30-50%: Moderate consideration, significant barriers exist
  • <30%: Low consideration, major perception issues to address
Business Implications:
  • At 43.67%, consideration is in the moderate range, indicating significant barriers to conversion from awareness to consideration.
  • The +2.67pp increase shows a positive trend that should be reinforced.
  • This suggests a significant gap between awareness (86.89%) and consideration (43.67%), indicating that while many Qatari travelers know about Abu Dhabi, fewer are actively considering it as a destination.
  • Marketing should focus on addressing the specific barriers that prevent aware consumers from considering Abu Dhabi, particularly the lack of direct flights (41%) and value perception (33%).
  • The moderate consideration rate indicates a need for targeted messaging that addresses the specific concerns and preferences of the Qatar market.
  • The positive growth trend (+2.67pp) suggests that current marketing efforts are having some success in improving consideration, but there is still significant room for improvement.

Intent

22.97% (+4.97pp)

Intent measures the percentage of considering consumers who intend to visit Abu Dhabi in the next 12 months.

How It's Calculated:

Based on survey responses to questions like "Which of the below holiday destinations are you likely to visit in the next 12 months?" The percentage represents those who respond positively out of those who are considering Abu Dhabi.

Interpretation:
  • 50%+: Excellent intent, very strong conversion potential
  • 30-50%: Good intent, healthy conversion potential
  • 20-30%: Moderate intent, conversion challenges exist
  • <20%: Low intent, significant conversion barriers
Business Implications:
  • At 22.97%, intent is in the moderate range, indicating some barriers to conversion from consideration to intent.
  • The +4.97pp increase shows a strong positive trend that should be leveraged.
  • This suggests that among those who consider Abu Dhabi, a moderate proportion have immediate plans to visit.
  • The significant increase in intent (+4.97pp) indicates that marketing efforts are successfully creating urgency and addressing practical barriers to booking.
  • Marketing should continue to focus on creating urgency and addressing practical barriers to booking (visa process, flight availability, etc.).
  • The moderate intent level with strong positive growth suggests that tactical, conversion-focused campaigns are working and should be continued to maintain this momentum.

Intent/Consideration Ratio

0.53 (+0.09)

This ratio measures the efficiency of converting consideration to intent, indicating how effectively we move consumers down the funnel.

How It's Calculated:

Intent percentage divided by Consideration percentage. For example: 22.97% ÷ 43.67% = 0.53

Interpretation:
  • 0.8+: Excellent conversion efficiency, minimal drop-off
  • 0.6-0.8: Good conversion efficiency
  • 0.4-0.6: Moderate conversion efficiency, significant drop-off
  • <0.4: Poor conversion efficiency, major funnel leakage
Business Implications:
  • At 0.53, the conversion from consideration to intent is moderate, indicating that 53% of those who consider Abu Dhabi intend to visit in the next 12 months.
  • The increase of +0.09 suggests a significant improvement in conversion efficiency.
  • This indicates that while both consideration and intent are increasing, intent is increasing at a faster rate, resulting in an improved ratio.
  • The moderate ratio suggests that once Qatari travelers consider Abu Dhabi, they have a reasonable likelihood of converting to actual intent to visit.
  • Marketing should focus on understanding and addressing the specific barriers that are preventing consideration from converting to intent, particularly the perception that there are no direct flights (41%).
  • The significant improvement in this ratio (+0.09) is a positive sign that should be leveraged in future marketing efforts.

Market Characteristics

Market characteristics describe fundamental travel behaviors and patterns specific to the Qatar market. These metrics help inform tactical marketing decisions and campaign timing.

Average Length of Stay

2.0 days

The average number of nights Qatari travelers spend in Abu Dhabi per visit.

How It's Calculated:

Based on hotel guest data and visitor surveys (DVS), calculated as the sum of all nights stayed divided by the number of visitors.

Business Implications:
  • The 2.0-day average stay indicates a very short vacation timeframe, typical of weekend getaways or short breaks.
  • This suggests marketing should focus on compact itineraries and experiences that can be enjoyed within a 2-day timeframe.
  • Package offerings should be designed around this timeframe to maximize appeal, with a focus on "weekend escape" packages.
  • There's an opportunity to develop weekend packages that include Abu Dhabi as a short break destination for Qatari travelers.
  • Hotel partners should be encouraged to develop 2-day packages with all-inclusive options to appeal to this market.
  • The very short stay suggests a need for high-impact experiences that deliver maximum value in a limited time, focusing on the most iconic attractions and experiences.

Booking Window

30 days

The average time between booking and arrival for Qatari travelers visiting Abu Dhabi.

How It's Calculated:

Based on booking data from hotels, flight booking engines and OTAs, calculated as the average number of days between booking confirmation and arrival date.

Business Implications:
  • The 30-day booking window is relatively short, indicating a market that makes travel decisions with moderate lead time.
  • Marketing campaigns should be timed to run approximately 1-2 months before target travel periods.
  • This window allows for some decision-making and comparison shopping by travelers, but suggests a more spontaneous booking behavior compared to other markets.
  • Last-minute offers and tactical promotions are likely to be effective for this market.
  • The moderate booking window suggests that digital media should be prioritized over traditional media, as there is limited time for consumers to respond to marketing messages.
  • This shorter planning period also suggests a need for streamlined booking processes and instant confirmation to facilitate quicker decision-making.
Strategic Example:

For the July-August peak season, major campaign pushes should begin in June to align with the 30-day booking window. Last-minute deals offered 15-20 days in advance could be particularly effective for this market, encouraging spontaneous travel decisions for weekend getaways.

Peak Months (Hotel Guests)

Aug & Jul

The months with the highest volume of Qatari hotel guests in Abu Dhabi.

How It's Calculated:

Based on hotel occupancy data, identifying the months with the highest number of Qatari guests.

Business Implications:
  • August (7,413 guests) and July (5,738 guests) represent the clear peak season for Qatari travelers.
  • This summer peak aligns with school holidays and the desire to escape Qatar's extreme summer heat.
  • Marketing should ensure sufficient inventory and capacity during these months.
  • Pricing strategies can be optimized for these high-demand periods.
  • There's an opportunity to develop shoulder season campaigns (June, September) to extend the peak periods.
  • The strong summer performance suggests a focus on indoor activities, luxury shopping, and air-conditioned attractions would resonate with this market during the hottest months.

Advanced Statistical Metrics

These advanced metrics provide deeper insights into market dynamics, helping to quantify relationships between marketing activities and outcomes, forecast accuracy, and market behavior patterns.

Seasonality Index

1.7 (moderate seasonal variation)

A statistical measure that quantifies the degree of seasonal variation in the Qatar travel market data.

How It's Calculated:

Typically calculated by dividing the highest monthly value by the average monthly value in a year. For example, if December has a travel query index of 9.62 (the highest month) and the average monthly index is around 5.66, the seasonality index would be approximately 1.7 (9.62 ÷ 5.66).

Interpretation:
  • 1.0-1.3: Low seasonal variation (relatively flat demand throughout the year)
  • 1.3-1.6: Moderate seasonal variation (noticeable peaks and troughs)
  • 1.6-2.0: High seasonal variation (significant peaks and troughs)
  • 2.0+: Extreme seasonal variation (very pronounced peaks and low seasons)
Business Implications:
  • The 1.7 value indicates that the market has high seasonal fluctuations, with peak months having about 70% higher travel query activity than the average month.
  • This is significant enough to warrant strong seasonal marketing strategies.
  • Marketing efforts should be concentrated before peak periods (considering the 30-day booking window).
  • There's also opportunity to grow travel interest during off-peak months through targeted campaigns.
  • Resource allocation should follow this seasonal pattern, with increased investment before peak periods.
  • The high seasonality suggests a need for differential pricing and promotion strategies across the year.

Media Elasticity

0.45 (moderate-high impact)

A marketing metric that measures how responsive travel queries are to changes in media impressions for the Qatar market.

How It's Calculated:

Calculated using regression analysis on historical data, comparing the percentage change in travel queries to the percentage change in media impressions over time.

Formula: Media Elasticity = % Change in Travel Queries ÷ % Change in Media Impressions

Interpretation:
  • <0.3: Low impact (minimal response to media investment)
  • 0.3-0.6: Moderate impact (reasonable response to media investment)
  • 0.6-1.0: High impact (strong response to media investment)
  • >1.0: Very high impact (exceptional response to media investment)
Business Implications:
  • The 0.45 elasticity means that for every 10% increase in media impressions, travel queries increase by approximately 4.5%.
  • This indicates that media investments drive travel queries with moderate-to-good efficiency.
  • The relationship is positive and relatively strong (in the upper half of the moderate range).
  • Media campaigns will generate positive ROI with good efficiency.
  • Doubling media spend will not double travel queries, but will produce meaningful growth.
  • The moderate-high elasticity suggests that the Qatar market is quite responsive to media campaigns, making it a good candidate for increased media investment.
  • This responsiveness suggests that the planned 86.93% increase in media impressions for 2025 should drive significant growth in travel queries.
Strategic Example:

If you're planning to increase media impressions by 86.93% for 2025 (as currently planned), you can expect approximately a 39.1% increase in travel queries (86.93% × 0.45 = 39.1%). This helps set realistic expectations for campaign performance and ROI calculations, and aligns well with the 40.5% growth forecast for the Qatar market.

YoY Growth Consistency

0.78 (high consistency)

A statistical measure that evaluates how uniform or predictable the year-over-year growth patterns are for the Qatar travel market.

How It's Calculated:

Typically calculated using a statistical measure like the coefficient of variation (inverse) or correlation coefficient of YoY growth rates across different time periods. The scale ranges from 0 to 1, where 0 represents completely inconsistent growth (highly volatile) and 1 represents perfectly consistent growth (same growth rate across all periods).

Interpretation:
  • <0.5: Low consistency (high volatility, unpredictable patterns)
  • 0.5-0.7: Moderate consistency (some predictability with variations)
  • 0.7-0.9: High consistency (reliable patterns with minor variations)
  • >0.9: Very high consistency (highly predictable patterns)
Business Implications:
  • The 0.78 consistency indicates that there is high predictability in how the Qatar market behaves year-over-year.
  • Forecasting can be done with high confidence.
  • Most months will show similar growth patterns to previous years.
  • Only occasional months may show unexpected variations requiring adaptive strategies.
  • Marketing teams can plan with greater certainty about expected outcomes.
  • Historical patterns provide reliable guidance for future planning.
  • This high consistency (0.78) contributes to a relatively narrow forecast confidence interval of ±7.2%.

Forecast Confidence Interval

±7.2%

A statistical measure indicating the range within which we expect the actual 2025 travel query values to fall with a certain level of confidence (typically 95%).

How It's Calculated:

Derived from statistical analysis of historical forecast accuracy, market volatility, and model parameters. It represents the standard error of the forecast multiplied by a factor corresponding to the desired confidence level (typically 1.96 for 95% confidence).

Interpretation:
  • <5%: Very high forecast precision, highly reliable predictions
  • 5-10%: Good forecast precision, reliable predictions with moderate variance
  • 10-15%: Moderate forecast precision, usable predictions with significant variance
  • >15%: Low forecast precision, high uncertainty in predictions
Business Implications:
  • The ±7.2% confidence interval means that if we forecast a travel query index of 150.0 for a particular month, the actual value is expected to fall between 139.2 and 160.8 with 95% confidence.
  • This relatively narrow interval indicates a reliable forecast for the Qatar market.
  • Marketing teams should plan with appropriate contingencies, knowing the potential range of outcomes.
  • Budget allocations should include flexibility to adjust based on actual performance within this range.
  • The confidence interval suggests that forecasts can be used with good confidence for planning purposes.
  • The good precision level aligns with the high YoY growth consistency (0.78), providing further validation of the forecast reliability.

Barriers to Visitation

Understanding why potential visitors choose not to travel to Abu Dhabi is crucial for developing targeted marketing strategies that address specific concerns and misconceptions.

Top Barriers to Visit

5 key factors

The primary reasons Qatar travelers give for not considering Abu Dhabi as a travel destination, based on survey research.

How It's Calculated:

Based on survey responses to questions like "What are the main reasons you would not consider traveling to Abu Dhabi?" The percentages represent the proportion of respondents who selected each reason.

Current Barriers (Q4 2024 vs Q4 2023):
  • No Direct Flights: 41% (+8pp) cite lack of direct flights
  • Value for Money: 33% (-1pp) say it's not value for money
  • Only for Younger People: 22% (0pp) think it's only for younger people
  • Distance Perception: 21% (+19pp) say it's too far to travel
  • Not Much to Do: 20% (-2pp) feel there's not much to do there
Business Implications:
  • No Direct Flights (41%, +8pp): This significant increase indicates growing frustration with flight connectivity. Marketing should work with airlines to increase direct flight options and highlight existing connections.
  • Value for Money (33%, -1pp): While slightly improving, this remains a significant barrier. Marketing should emphasize the value proposition of Abu Dhabi experiences and develop special value packages for the Qatar market.
  • Only for Younger People (22%, 0pp): This perception has remained stable. Marketing should showcase experiences that appeal to all age groups and highlight family-friendly activities.
  • Distance Perception (21%, +19pp): This dramatic increase is concerning, especially given the close proximity of Qatar to Abu Dhabi. Marketing should emphasize the short flight time (under 1 hour) and ease of travel between the two destinations.
  • Not Much to Do (20%, -2pp): This slight improvement suggests that awareness of Abu Dhabi's attractions is growing, but more work is needed to showcase the diversity of experiences available.
Strategic Recommendation:

Based on these barriers, a "Quick Weekend Escape" campaign highlighting the short travel time, direct flight options, and value-packed 2-day itineraries would address three of the five top barriers to visitation from the Qatar market. This campaign should emphasize the diversity of experiences available for all age groups and showcase how much can be enjoyed in a short timeframe.

Increasing Barriers

3 worsening factors

Barriers to visitation that have shown significant deterioration over time, representing challenges that need to be addressed.

Worsening Barriers (Q4 2024 vs Q4 2023):
  • Distance Perception: 21% (+19pp) say it's too far to travel
  • No Direct Flights: 41% (+8pp) cite lack of direct flights
  • Cost Perception: 14% (+3pp) believe it is too expensive

  • The substantial increase in "Distance Perception" (+19pp) represents the most critical challenge, indicating a significant deterioration in perception of travel convenience that requires immediate attention.
  • The significant increase in "No Direct Flights" (+8pp) suggests growing frustration with flight connectivity, which may be contributing to the distance perception issue.
  • The moderate increase in "Cost Perception" (+3pp) indicates a growing concern about the affordability of Abu Dhabi as a destination.
  • These worsening barriers suggest a need for targeted communications that emphasize the ease and affordability of travel to Abu Dhabi from Qatar.
  • The combination of these barriers suggests that Qatar travelers are increasingly perceiving Abu Dhabi as less accessible and less affordable, despite its geographic proximity.
  • These worsening barriers should be prioritized in marketing communications, with particular focus on correcting the misperception about travel distance and highlighting affordable travel options.

  • The significant decrease in "Unfriendly Perception" (-7pp) suggests improvements in how the hospitality and friendliness of Abu Dhabi is perceived.
  • The notable decrease in "Bad Experiences" (-6pp) indicates improving satisfaction levels and positive word-of-mouth.
  • The slight decrease in "Not Much to Do" (-2pp) suggests growing awareness of the variety of experiences available in Abu Dhabi.
  • Marketing should leverage these positive changes, particularly the improvements in perceptions of family-friendliness and hospitality, in communications to the Qatar market.
  • These improving barriers demonstrate that perceptions can be changed through targeted communications and service improvements, providing a model for addressing the currently worsening barriers.

Conclusion

This guide provides a comprehensive explanation of the key metrics used in our market analysis for Qatar. By understanding these metrics and their business implications, DCT - Abu Dhabi can make more informed strategic marketing decisions.

The Qatar market shows several distinctive characteristics:

  • Very strong awareness (86.89%) with moderate consideration (43.67%) and intent (22.97%), indicating some barriers to conversion
  • A moderate intent-to-consideration ratio (0.53) indicating reasonable funnel conversion once consideration is achieved
  • A very short average stay (2.0 days) indicating weekend getaway vacation patterns
  • A short booking window (30 days) suggesting more spontaneous travel decisions
  • Bi-modal seasonality with peaks in travel queries in April and December, but hotel guest peaks in July and August
  • Moderate-high media elasticity (0.45) suggesting good response to media investments
  • Strong interest in theme parks and entertainment attractions, with Ferrari World and Sheikh Zayed Grand Mosque being the most searched attractions
  • Strong preference for the Dusit Thani Abu Dhabi, with luxury and upscale international chain hotels being preferred
  • Significant barriers including lack of direct flights (41%), value perception (33%), and a growing misperception about travel distance (21%, +19pp)
  • Improving perceptions of family-friendliness and hospitality, with significant decreases in concerns about children's entertainment (-7pp) and unfriendliness (-7pp)

These insights suggest a strategic focus on:

  • Addressing the significant gap between awareness and consideration through targeted messaging that addresses specific barriers
  • Correcting the misperception about travel distance through communications that emphasize the short flight time and ease of travel
  • Working with airlines to increase direct flight options and highlighting existing connections
  • Emphasizing the value proposition of Abu Dhabi experiences and developing special value packages for the Qatar market
  • Highlighting the family-friendly entertainment options on Yas Island, which align with the strong interest in theme parks and entertainment attractions
  • Developing compact itineraries that maximize the typical 2.0-day stay, focusing on high-impact experiences
  • Timing campaigns to align with the 30-day booking window, particularly for the summer peak period
  • Creating marketing content that showcases the blend of entertainment, cultural attractions, and shopping experiences available in Abu Dhabi
  • Developing partnerships with popular hotels like the Dusit Thani Abu Dhabi to create targeted offers for the Qatar market
  • Positioning Abu Dhabi as a convenient, value-packed weekend escape destination for Qatar travelers

With these insights and ongoing monitoring of these metrics, DCT - Abu Dhabi can formulate more effective marketing strategies and increase our return on marketing investment in the Qatar market. The significant increase in planned media impressions for 2025 (1.47B, +86.93% YoY) should drive substantial growth in travel queries, particularly for the December peak period.
